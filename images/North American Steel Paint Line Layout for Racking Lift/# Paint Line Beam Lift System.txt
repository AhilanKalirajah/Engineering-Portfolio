# Paint Line Beam Lift System

## Project Summary

Designed the foundation for a lifting solution to reduce manual handling of heavy paint line beams after workers experienced repetitive lifting injuries.

The first phase of the project involved recreating the entire paint line in AutoCAD to accurately understand the existing equipment, available space, elevations, and physical constraints before any lifting system concepts could be developed.

---

## The Problem

Operators were manually lifting heavy beams during the paint process, creating an ergonomic risk and increasing the potential for workplace injuries.

The objective was to understand the existing production line and create an accurate engineering layout that could later be used to design a safer lifting solution.

---

## My Responsibilities

- Surveyed the existing paint line.
- Created detailed AutoCAD layouts from field measurements.
- Produced plan, front, side, and cross-sectional views.
- Documented structural members, conveyor elevations, cooling tunnel, washer, and surrounding equipment.
- Identified design constraints that would influence the future lift design.
- Created engineering documentation that served as the foundation for the lifting system concept.

## Key Engineering Considerations

During the layout process, the following constraints were identified:

- Existing production equipment could not be relocated.
- Structural steel limited possible mounting locations.
- Conveyor elevations had to remain unchanged.
- Access for operators and maintenance personnel had to be maintained.
- The proposed lifting system could not interfere with normal production.

---

## Outcome

The completed AutoCAD layouts became the engineering foundation for developing a beam lifting solution aimed at reducing manual handling and improving operator safety.

By accurately documenting the existing production line before beginning design work, the project reduced uncertainty and provided a reliable basis for future concept development.
