# Safety Clip Development

## Project Summary

Developed a production-ready replacement safety clip by reverse engineering an existing shop-floor component into a fully parametric SolidWorks model.

The project included prototype development using 3D printing to validate fit and function before releasing the design for production.

---

## Project Objective

A replacement safety clip was required for an existing racking system, but no usable CAD model existed.

The objective was to accurately recreate the component in SolidWorks, verify the design through physical prototypes, and produce engineering documentation for manufacturing.

---

## My Responsibilities

- Reverse engineered an existing safety clip.
- Created a fully parametric SolidWorks model.
- Produced manufacturing drawings.
- Generated multiple 3D printed prototypes.
- Verified fit and functionality on the production racking.
- Updated the design based on prototype testing.
- Released the final design for mass production.

---

## Engineering Process

### 1. Reverse Engineer Existing Component

An existing production safety clip was measured and analyzed to understand its geometry, spring features, bends, and overall function.

---

### 2. Create CAD Model

The component was recreated as a fully parametric SolidWorks sheet metal model that accurately reflected the production part.

---

### 3. Prototype Validation

Multiple prototypes were produced using a 3D printer and test-fitted on the production racking to verify:

- Fit
- Retention
- Ease of installation
- Functionality

Necessary adjustments were incorporated before final release.

---

### 4. Production Release

Once validated, the engineering drawing was completed and the design was approved for mass production.
